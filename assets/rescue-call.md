# rescue-call.md — Dormant Trial Rescue Script

**Who this is for:** The 19 dormant trials. They signed up, so the "do I want this" objection is already dead. They stalled at activation — almost always call forwarding (confusing, scary, or "I'll do it later"). They've received 2 emails (re-engagement + apology). This call is the third touch and it's the founder calling personally.

**Goal of the call:** Forwarding turned ON before you hang up. Not a promise. Not a scheduled follow-up. Live activation, witnessed first call. "Later" is how they became dormant.

**Mindset:** You are not selling. You are onboarding someone who already bought the idea. Tone = helpful founder doing white-glove setup, mildly apologetic about the rough patch, confident the product is ready now.

---

## Opener (first 15 seconds)

> "Hey [Name], it's [You] — the founder of Argora Voice, the AI receptionist you signed up for a few weeks back. Not a sales call — you're already in. I'm calling because I owe you a working product. We hit some bumps when you first tried it, I've personally rebuilt the whole thing this month, and I'm calling every early signup to set them up myself. You got ten minutes right now? I'll have it answering your calls before we hang up."

Why it works:
- "Founder" + "not a sales call" drops their guard instantly
- Owns the past failure without dwelling (matches the apology email they got)
- "Ten minutes right now" forces the live-activation frame from sentence one

If they're busy: **do not accept "email me."**
> "Totally get it. When's a real ten minutes today or tomorrow — and I mean a time you'll actually be near your phone, because the whole setup happens live on this call. Morning or afternoon?"

Lock a specific time. Calendar invite + SMS reminder immediately.

---

## The pivot (their stall, named for them)

After any small talk:

> "Can I guess what happened? You signed up, poked around, and the call-forwarding step felt like a thing — star codes, 'what if it answers weird,' all that. That's where almost everyone stalled, and honestly that was on us for making it feel like homework. Here's the fix: we do it together, right now, and we start in **overflow mode** — Argora only picks up when you're busy or miss the call. Your phone works exactly like it does today. The only difference is voicemail stops eating your customers."

Overflow mode is the entire unlock. It reframes activation from *handing over your phone* to *installing a safety net*. Nothing about their day changes until a call would have been missed anyway.

---

## Live setup (the core 5 minutes)

1. "What kind of phone rings when a customer calls — cell or office line? Which carrier?"
2. Walk them through conditional forwarding (busy/no-answer) for their carrier. You read the star code, they dial it, done in 60 seconds.
3. **The magic moment — do not skip:** "Okay. Now don't answer your next call. I'm dialing your business number right now. Just listen."
4. Call their line from your phone. Let them hear Argora answer *their* business, ask them a real question ("Do you guys do emergency weekend calls?"), let it offer to book you.
5. Silence. Let them react. This is the moment the product sells itself — say nothing until they do.

Then:
> "That's what every after-hours and lunch-rush caller gets from now on, instead of voicemail. And you just got the text summary — check your phone."

---

## The close

> "So here's the deal I'm giving every early signup who goes live before August 1: **$199 a month, locked for 12 months** — it's $500 after that date, no exceptions, because the founding group is how I get my case studies. Thirty-day guarantee: if Argora doesn't book you real appointments in the first month, you don't pay. You're already set up as of two minutes ago. Want me to lock the rate in?"

- The product is already working when you ask. They're not buying a promise, they're keeping something they can hear.
- Deadline is real and stated plainly. Guarantee kills the last bit of risk.
- If they need to think: "Totally fine — forwarding stays on, trial runs to Aug 6 either way. But the $199 lock dies August 1 whether you've decided or not. I'll text you a reminder three days before."

## Objection quick-fires

**"What if it says something wrong to a customer?"**
> "In overflow mode it only ever talks to people who were about to hit your voicemail — the bar it has to beat is a beep. And you get a transcript of every call, so if it ever handles something in a way you don't like, tell me and I tune it same-day. Early customers get me personally, that's the trade."

**"My front desk / wife / I answer the phones fine."**
> "During hours, sure — and nothing changes there. What answered your phone last night at 9pm? [pause] Right. That caller is who this is for. Argora's competing with your voicemail, not your people."

**"I'll set it up myself later."**
> "That's what the last three weeks were, though, right? [light laugh] No judgment — it's exactly why I'm calling instead of emailing. It's four minutes and I'm already on the line. Let's just knock it out."

**"$199 is a lot."**
> "One saved [patient/service call/client] is $[their avg value]. If Argora books one appointment a month you'd have missed, it's paid for itself [X] times. And if it doesn't book you anything in 30 days, it's free. You're risking zero."

**"Is this thing actually reliable now?"**
> "Fair question — you saw the rough version. I rebuilt the voice server this month, added downtime alerts that page me by text, and you just heard it answer your own business live. That's the honest answer: it wasn't ready before. It is now, and you have 30 days to prove me wrong at my expense."

## After every call — log in pipeline.csv

- `activated` — forwarding on, heard the live call → send first-booking watch, testimonial-ask queues on first real booking
- `scheduled` — locked a specific setup time → calendar invite + SMS reminder same minute
- `soft-no` — not now, didn't cancel → one SMS on July 29: "48 hours left on the $199 lock — 4-minute setup, I'll do it with you"
- `dead` — cancelled/wrong fit → mark closed, note the reason verbatim (this is roadmap gold)

## Cadence

- Work dormants top-of-list in every 7:30–9:00 call block until the list is empty. Cold leads wait.
- No answer → voicemail (below) + same-day SMS. Second attempt next business day at a different hour. Three no-contacts → soft-no track.

**Voicemail (under 20 seconds):**
> "Hey [Name], [You] here — founder of Argora Voice, the AI receptionist you signed up for. I rebuilt it, it's working, and I'm personally setting up every early signup this week — takes four minutes and I do it with you on the phone. Call or text me back at [number] and grab the $199 founding rate before it's gone August 1."

**Same-day SMS:**
> "Hey [Name] — [You] from Argora Voice (just left a VM). 4-min setup, done live with you on the phone, $199/mo locked before Aug 1. When's good — today or tomorrow?"
